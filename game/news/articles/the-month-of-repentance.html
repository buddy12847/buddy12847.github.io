The Month of Repentance

R1-GY3
Ian Dains

Today marks the first day of the month of Repentance, fifteen days for reflection over the past year as Garfunkel Year Three comes to an end and Garfunkel Year Four looms over the horizon.
Despite the name of the month, I believe that the month of Repentance is about much more than repenting for one's sins, and more about looking at one's successes and failures over the past year to become more aware of where one can improve and to be a better and more successful person over the following year.

The month of repentance is an opportunity for us all to take responsibility for our lives, and to make an effort to improve. I encourage you to make a pledge with yourself to address where you wish to improve, and to make that pledge happen, even when it gets difficult. Because you either fight to make your life better, or you live like Cooper Hobbs, no ambition, and no future, hardly a life worth living at all. 

As is the spirit of the month, I would like to take this opportunity to say how I wish to improve my life in GY4. Over the past year I have many times dropped Japanese study, only to start again for a week or so and then stopping once more, so my pledge to myself in this coming year is to stay motivated and consistent with Japanese studies, and as Garfunkel is my witness, I will.
